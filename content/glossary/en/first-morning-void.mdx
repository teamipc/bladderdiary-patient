The **first morning void (FMV)** is the urine you pass when you naturally wake for the day. It's a specific entry in a 3-day [bladder diary](/learn/bladder-diary) because of how the math behind [nocturia](/learn/nocturia) works.

In nocturia accounting, the FMV is **counted toward the nighttime total**, not the daytime total. The reason is mechanical: that urine was produced during the hours you were asleep. It just happened to come out after you woke up. The [nocturnal polyuria index](/learn/glossary/nocturnal-polyuria-index), which decides whether your nocturia is from the kidneys or the bladder, includes the FMV in the nighttime numerator.

The FMV is also typically your **[maximum voided volume](/learn/glossary/maximum-voided-volume)** of the day. After 6 to 8 hours without voiding, the bladder fills to its functional capacity, and the FMV is often around 400 to 500 mL in a healthy adult. A small FMV (under 300 mL) is a meaningful clue:

- Either the bladder couldn't hold that long, which points to a storage problem.
- Or you woke up before your bladder was full, which suggests a different sleep disturbance.

A few rules for the diary:

- The FMV is the void after you naturally wake, not a void you took because you woke up wanting to pee at 4am. The 4am void is a [nocturia](/learn/glossary/nocturia) episode.
- A bedtime void, just before sleep onset, is also not part of the night. It's the last daytime void.

Done well, those two boundaries (bedtime void and FMV) frame the night cleanly. Without them, the [nocturnal polyuria](/learn/glossary/nocturnal-polyuria) calculation breaks.
