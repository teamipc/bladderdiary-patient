- **D-04 — Word counting convention for Chinese.** Chinese text does not whitespace-split into words; the natural counting unit is the character. For the SEO purpose (Google's "thin content" signal), what matters is the rendered character/glyph density per page, which Google estimates via tokenization, not whitespace counting. Two viable rules:
  - (a) Apply a character-to-word equivalence: a Chinese 字 corresponds roughly to 0.6 to 1 English word in dense informational content. So the ZH intro needs roughly 600 to 1000 characters (汉字) to deliver the same content density as 600 English words.
  - (b) Apply a content-equivalence rule: the ZH intro covers the same logical structure as the EN intro (same number of paragraphs, same conceptual coverage), at locale-natural density.

  **CHOSEN: (b) content-equivalence + a 1000-character floor for ZH.** The ZH intro should cover the same 3-5 logical sections as the EN intro, at native-Mandarin density. A 1000-character floor (counted via `[...str].length` minus whitespace) ensures the page has substantive content even by character-count metrics. The verification step in 12-04 will assert this character floor for ZH specifically.

  For AR: whitespace-split is the natural word-count convention; >= 600 words by `.split(/\s+/).length` per the same logic as the LTR locales.

- **D-05 — Tone + voice constraints (apply uniformly across all 6 locales):**
  - No em-dashes (per `feedback_no_em_dashes` memory + `content/README.md` line 318)
  - Collaborative, peer-direct tone (per `feedback_collaborative_tone`) — not authoritative, not clinical-as-recipient
  - For the for-women intro: do NOT center urologists as the default clinician (per `feedback_dont_center_urologists`). Mention PFPT, urogynecologist, OR-PT, primary care, urologist — multiple options, not "your urologist."
  - IPC positioning honesty (per `project_ipc_positioning`): IPC's clinical methodology is centered on men's pelvic health education, but the diary tool itself is universally usable. The for-women intro should NOT pretend IPC is gender-neutral — it should acknowledge the asymmetry while emphasizing the tool's universality (the current EN intro does this well; preserve the framing).
  - The audience-landing intros are PATIENT-facing copy (not clinician-facing). The reader is the patient who wants to USE the diary, not the clinician who's evaluating it.

- **D-06 — Logical structure of each expanded intro.** Aim for 5 paragraphs (separated by `\n\n` inside the JSON string) covering:
  1. **What this audience experiences** (concrete: which symptoms, when in life, why they often get misattributed) — ~120-150 words
  2. **What the articles below cover** (a 1-2-sentence preview of each major topic the section addresses, with implicit internal-link signal via topic naming) — ~150 words
  3. **What the 3-day bladder diary will reveal** (the specific clinical data the tool captures: frequency, volume, fluid intake, urgency, leaks, nocturia) and what that data means for the audience — ~120-150 words
  4. **Who the right clinician is** (PFPT, urogynecologist, urologist, PCP — multiple options, not gate-keeping) — ~100-120 words
  5. **The path forward / CTA setup** (priming the diary CTA below without restating it) — ~80-100 words

  Total per intro: ~570-670 words. EN target: aim for ~650 words to give margin. ZH target: 1000+ characters. Other locales: >= 600 words by `.split(/\s+/).length`.
